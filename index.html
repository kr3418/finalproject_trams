NYC Tram Potential Map

This project explores how modern tram lines could complement New York City’s existing MTA network. Rather than replacing subway service, trams can function as a “missing middle” transit mode that improves local access and crosstown connections, providing higher-capacity and more reliable service along congested and overcrowded bus corridors.

New York city is already beginning to reclaim street space through initiatives like busways (i.e 14 street and soon Flatbush avenue). In many cases, modern tram infrastructure could represent a long term evolution of these investments by introducing higher quality mobility for surface transit.
